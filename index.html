Cats and Dogs

History of Cats and Dogs

  • Dogs originally evolved from gray wolves. Humans encountered gray wolves during their migrations and gradually domesticated them into modern dogs by selectively breeding gentle individuals and using them to help humans.
  • Domestic cats are generally believed to have originated from the African wildcat (Felis silvestris lybica). Cats were domesticated later than dogs, beginning approximately 6,000 years ago in the agricultural society of ancient Egypt. With the development of agriculture, granaries attracted large numbers of rats, and wild cats followed. Humans discovered that cats were effective at catching rats, so they began to keep them in their homes to protect food.

How to raise Cats and Dogs

  • About cat:Raising a kitten requires providing a safe and comfortable home environment, choosing age-appropriate cat food and water, and paying attention to regular health checks, deworming, and vaccinations. In addition, socialization training should be carried out from an early age, and good living habits should be cultivated, such as using cat litter, combing hair, trimming nails, and brushing teeth.
  • About dog:It is necessary to pay attention to diet, living environment, health management and training. In the early stage of returning home, the puppy should be provided with a quiet and comfortable environment, and fed with small amounts of frequent meals. At the same time, it should be kept warm and avoid frequent bathing. Ensuring that it is vaccinated on time and dewormed regularly is the key to its health. Both indoor and outdoor environments should be managed safely.

About Cats and Dogs food

  • About cat food：Cats are carnivores, and their staple food should be high in animal protein, such as cooked chicken, fish, and beef, and provided with adequate water. In addition, a small amount of cooked vegetables (such as pumpkin and carrots), egg yolks, and cat food are also acceptable, but care should be taken to avoid high carbohydrates and processed seasonings.
  • About dog food：Cats are carnivores, and their staple food should be high in animal protein, such as cooked chicken, fish, and beef, and provided with adequate water. In addition, a small amount of cooked vegetables (such as pumpkin and carrots)， egg yolks, and cat food are also acceptable, but care should be taken to avoid high carbohydrates and processed seasonings.
